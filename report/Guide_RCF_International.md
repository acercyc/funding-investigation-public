## 1. Executive Overview

The **Research Council of Finland (RCF)** places international cooperation at the core of its strategy to enhance the quality, impact, and renewal of Finnish research. Through bilateral and multilateral agreements, RCF facilitates joint research projects, researcher mobility, and participation in global research networks.

### Key Facts at a Glance

| Attribute | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Strategic Priority** | Internationalization is embedded in all RCF funding opportunities |
| **Funding Model** | Co-funding with international partners (typically 50-50 or Lead Agency model) |
| **Typical Funding Amount** | **€300,000 – €500,000** for 3-year bilateral projects (Finnish side) |
| **Main Website** | [aka.fi/en/from-research-to-society/rcf-impact/international-cooperation/](https://www.aka.fi/en/from-research-to-society/rcf-impact/international-cooperation/) |
| **Policy Document** | [RCF Policy for Promoting International Cooperation (PDF)](https://www.aka.fi/globalassets/2-suomen-akatemian-toiminta/1-mita-teemme/6-kansainvalinen-toiminta/sa-kv-linjaus-en-final-valmis.pdf) |

### Three-Tiered Cooperation Structure

1.  **Nordic Cooperation:** Regional collaboration through NordForsk and Nordic funding agencies
2.  **European Cooperation:** Participation in ERA-NET, CHIST-ERA, Horizon Europe partnerships
3.  **Global Cooperation:** Bilateral agreements with key countries (USA, Japan, China, India, Brazil, etc.)

## 2. Policy Framework & Strategic Objectives

RCF's international cooperation policy emphasizes five core objectives:

### 2.1 Strategic Objectives

| Objective | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Scientific Renewal** | Support breakthroughs and scientific renewal through international research cooperation |
| **Global Visibility** | Enhance the global visibility of Finnish scientific research |
| **EU Success** | Improve success rates in European Union RDI funding calls |
| **Talent Attraction** | Attract high-level experts from abroad to Finland |
| **International Standards** | Align Finnish scientific community with international standards in scientific autonomy, responsible research, democracy, and human rights |

### 2.2 Key Principles

*   **All funding opportunities** are designed with internationalization in mind
*   **Bilateral agreements** facilitate joint funding and researcher mobility
*   **Lead Agency model** simplifies application processes (single proposal, dual funding)
*   **Multilateral networks** address global challenges through coordinated action

## 7. Application Process & Timeline

### 7.1 General Application Process

| Step | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Identify Opportunity** | Monitor RCF website and partner agency announcements |
| **2. Find International Partner** | Establish collaboration agreement with partner country researcher |
| **3. Prepare Joint Proposal** | Coordinate with international partner to develop unified research plan |
| **4. Submit to Both Agencies** | Submit to RCF (Finnish side) and partner agency (international side) |
| **5. Review Process** | Both agencies review; funding decisions coordinated |
| **6. Funding Start** | Typically 6-12 months after application deadline |

### 7.2 Lead Agency Model (USA, Some European Calls)

| Step | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Submit to Lead Agency** | Single proposal submitted to lead agency (e.g., NSF) |
| **2. Submit to RCF** | Finnish applicants submit version to RCF |
| **3. Coordinated Review** | Lead agency conducts review; RCF accepts results |
| **4. Joint Funding** | Both agencies fund their respective partners |

### 7.3 Typical Timeline

| Stage | Timeline |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Call Announcement** | Varies (typically 3-6 months before deadline) |
| **Application Deadline** | Varies by call |
| **Review Period** | 4-8 months |
| **Funding Decision** | Typically 6-12 months after deadline |
| **Project Start** | 1-3 months after decision |

### 8.4 Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1.  **Starting Too Late:** International collaborations require 6-12 months of relationship-building. Start now.

5.  **Missing Deadlines:** International calls often have different deadlines for each partner country.
