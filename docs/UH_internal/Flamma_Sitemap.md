### 2. Research Funding Databases

**URL:** https://flamma.helsinki.fi/en/group/tutkimuksen-tuki/rahoitustietokannat

#### Summary
Page providing information about two research funding databases available to University of Helsinki researchers: Research Professional (commercial database with institutional access) and Research.fi (free public database).

#### Sections

1. **Research Professional** (Main section)
   - Description: Commercial, online research funding opportunities database and alerting service with broad coverage of international (including EU's Horizon Europe) and national funding opportunities.
   - Access: University of Helsinki has access to Research Professional funding database Standard functionality.
   - Access methods:
     - Campus log in at researchprofessional.com (Note: If using OpenVPN, use the full `hy-vpn-allroute` profile, not `hy-vpn-helsinkifi-only`)
     - Personal log in via Haka-federation using University of Helsinki credentials (enables browsing outside university network)
   - Features:
     - Browse the funding database
     - Create customised searches and share with colleagues
     - Weekly email alerts of new funding opportunities matching criteria
     - Save and share funding opportunities
   - License: Use only for UH related purposes
   
   **Expandable Sections:**
   
   - **Getting started with Research Professional:**
     - Access options:
       - Campus Access: If on campus, taken directly to "Home" page
       - Log in with university account (SSO): Works outside campus too
     - Log in process:
       1. Select University of Helsinki in drop-down list and click Log in
       2. Redirected to UH (Haka-federation) sign-in page
       3. Three options on Link Accounts page (required only once):
         - Create new account: If you don't have a Research Professional account
         - Link to existing account: If you already have an account
         - Continue as a guest: Limited functionality
     - Includes video tutorial (1 minute, 4 seconds)
   
   - **Training and guidance:**
     - Online demos: Research Professional provides regular online demos (register on their website, choose a standard session)
     - Research Professional on YouTube: Video tutorials available on their YouTube channel
     - Research Professional user guides: Can be downloaded from their website

2. **Research.fi database** (Main section)
   - Description: Free research funding database open to everyone, no charge. Can be used for searching funding opportunities in sciences, arts and culture, for both project and personal grants.
   - Coverage: Includes Finnish and selected foreign funding opportunities available for Finns or those residing in Finland
   - Updates: Funding organisations listed and new opportunities added constantly
   - Access: Available at https://research.fi/en/results/funding-calls?status=open

### 3. National Research Funding

**URL:** https://flamma.helsinki.fi/en/group/tutkimuksen-tuki/kansallinen-tutkimusrahoitus

#### Summary
Overview page for national research funding sources in Finland. Provides guidance on applying for funding from national sources, including the Research Council of Finland, Business Finland, national foundations, and state funding. Emphasizes the need to contact units, Research Funding Services, and Financial Services well in advance when applying.

#### Sections

1. **Introduction**
   - Guidance: Contact your own unit, Research Funding Services and Financial Services well in advance when applying for funding from national sources
   - Risk assessment: If application includes international collaboration, complete mandatory risk assessment (link to instructions)

2. **Research Council of Finland**
   - Who can apply: Researchers, research groups, and organizations
   - Funding types:
     - Salary and research costs
     - Researchers' mobility
     - Projects
     - Centres of Excellence
     - Joint international projects
     - Research infrastructures (acquisition, establishment, upgrading, expansion)
     - Strengthening university's profile
     - Forming Flagship clusters

3. **Strategic Research Funding**
   - Purpose: For large, multidisciplinary consortia emphasizing continuous interaction with information users
   - Decision maker: Strategic Research Council (SRC)

4. **Business Finland**
   - Purpose: Finances cooperation and joint projects of research organizations and companies
   - Funding for:
     - Development of research ideas
     - Development of new knowledge and innovations
     - Preparation of commercialization of research ideas

5. **Other National Research Funding**
   - National foundations: Provide personal grants for purposes determined by their rules; many also provide funding to research groups
   - State Council: Grants funding for various investigation and research activities
   - Ministries and state aid: Use of funding primarily regulated by State Aid Act
   - Joint EU-national funding: Research Council of Finland, Business Finland, and Ministries provide funding for projects jointly funded by EU and national bodies

### 3.1. Research Council of Finland

**URL:** https://flamma.helsinki.fi/en/group/tutkimuksen-tuki/suomen-akatemia

#### Summary
Comprehensive guide for applying to the Research Council of Finland (RCF). Includes detailed proposal process guidelines, information about different funding types (Spark, Forge, Shift, SRC), budgeting instructions, mobility funding, and project management guidance.

#### Sections

1. **Introduction**
   - Who can apply: Researchers, research groups, and organizations
   - Funding types: Salary, research costs, researchers' mobility
   - Links to RCF website for funding opportunities overview

2. **Calls Information**
   - Open and upcoming calls (link to RCF site)
   - Closed calls (link to RCF site)

3. **Proposal Process Guidelines** (Detailed checklist)
   - Always check call text and guidelines
   - Respect deadlines
   - Check eligibility from call text
   - Familiarize with UH's guidance on planning research projects
   - Check scientific council policies, assessment guidelines, and previous funding decisions
   - Familiarize with RCF guidance on research security
   - Conduct mandatory risk assessment for international collaboration
   - Use risk assessment results for security attachment required by RCF
   - Tips for writing successful research plan (PDF 2026)
   - UH's internal process for Data Management Plans
   - Contact financial services for budget advice
   - Fill out Funding application form in SAP Fiori (for dean/head approval)
   - Note UH internal deadline for Winter call budget approval (29 October 2025)
   - Submit application using RCF online service
   - Provide name of commitment issuer in e-form
   - RCF sends request for commitment by site of research after call closes

4. **Spark Funding for researchers** (Expandable)
   - Academy Project Funding
   - Academy Research Fellow Funding
   - Academy Professorship
   - Clinical Researchers Funding
   - Link to RCF Spark Funding pages

5. **Forge Funding for research environments** (Expandable)
   - Funds research environments strengthening cutting-edge science
   - **Finnish Flagships:** Flagship Programme supports high-quality research and economic/societal impact; UH internal guidance on Flagship Programme calls
   - **Research Infrastructures:** Funding for acquisition, establishment, upgrading of nationally significant research infrastructures; UH internal guidance on infrastructure calls
   - **Finnish Centres of Excellence (CoE):** Lead the way in science, 8-year funding term; Call schedule for 2024 CoE call: opened June 2024, letters of intent deadline 25 September 2024, first-stage decisions April 2025, full applications June 2025, selection October 2025, funding starts 1 January 2026; Links to CoE information and UH internal guidelines; Contact: Helena Holopainen (02941 24778, 050 3175 096)
   - **International Collaboration in High-Performance Computing:** Based on Finland's strategic partnerships in high-performance computing

6. **Shift Funding for solving societal challenges** (Expandable)
   - **Proof of Concept Funding:** Promotes utilization of research results toward economic and societal impact
   - **Funding for Clinical Research:** High-quality, ambitious research plans implemented by research team consortia
   - **Funding for University Profiles:** Strengthens university research profiles, supports strategic profiling; UH guidelines on applying for Profi funding
   - **International Funding Cooperation:** Supports internationalization of Finnish science; Nordic cooperation, European cooperation, Global cooperation; International calls, RCF policy for promoting international cooperation (PDF)
   - **Funding for EU Recovery and Resilience Facility (RRF):** Part of Sustainable Growth Programme for Finland
   - **Academy Programme Funding:** Thematic, target-oriented, coordinated research programmes; Currently open: Academy Programme for sport science and physical activity ACTIVE

7. **Strategic Research Council (SRC) funding** (Expandable)
   - Funds high-quality research with great societal relevance and impact
   - Seeks solutions to grand challenges requiring multidisciplinary approaches
   - Active collaboration between knowledge producers and users
   - SRC programmes run 3-6 years, divided into two three-year parts
   - Two-stage calls: Letter of Intent and Full Proposal
   - Further information links: Funding principles, Open calls, Programmes and projects, Strategic Research Funding, Themes, What's new
   - Instructions for applicants: 2026 call text published, Obtain unit head's approval via SAP Fiori, Mandatory attachments (Letter of Intent abstract and budget), SRC funding overview, Consortium structure, Research plan structure, FAQ, How to write interaction plan (PDF), Interaction plan guidance blog, Online services (SARA), Commitment by site of research, Letter of commitment by partners
   - Guidance for grantees: Consortium agreement/contracts (contact research lawyers: tutkimuksenjuristit@helsinki.fi), IPR issues in consortium agreement, Include project plan and budget in email to research lawyers, Societal Interaction of Science in SRC projects (PDF findings)

8. **Application budgeting** (Expandable)
   - Familiarize with call text, guidelines, and appendices
   - Contact project controllers for budget assistance
   - Project controllers check budget before submission
   - UH budget template (Excel) available (internal use only)
   - **Things to consider:** Can apply for researcher, research team, research costs, traveling costs, mobility, infrastructure
   - **Personnel costs and overheads:** Full cost model applies to most RCF funding; Academy Professor funding also uses full cost model from winter call 2025; Four-stage career path hierarchy for teaching and research staff; Contact HR coordinator and/or project controller for requirement levels
   - **Coefficients (CFO decision 1/2025):** Effective working hours: 87.5%; Indirect employee costs: 48%
   - **Overhead shares:** City Center Campus: 70%; Meilahti Campus, Viikki Campus, HiLIFE, LUOMUS: 115%; Kumpula Campus: 81%
   - **Other costs:** All research costs must be justified in research plan; Materials, supplies, traveling costs, services include VAT

9. **Budgeting mobility** (Expandable)
   - Mobility during funding period (Finnish or foreign research environment)
   - Minimum mobility period: two weeks
   - Mobility allowance for stays abroad longer than a month:
     - Within Europe: EUR 1,500/month (alone) / EUR 2,000/month (with family)
     - Outside Europe: EUR 1,800/month (alone) / EUR 2,300/month (with family)
     - High cost destinations: EUR 3,000/month (alone) / EUR 3,500/month (with family)
   - Higher mobility allowance requires family members to move and stay abroad
   - Family members: wedded/cohabiting partner, children (including adoptive/foster)
   - UH pays mobility allowance as fee, indirect costs and taxes subtracted
   - Budget mobility allowance and travel in travel costs
   - Personal costs (family tickets, moving) paid if booked through GBT Finland Oy and included in budget
   - Stay abroad longer than 30 days primarily interpreted as commission
   - Contact HR coordinator well in advance for work abroad
   - Faculty may support mobility with additional fee from basic funding

10. **Positive funding decisions and project launch** (Expandable)
    - Both beneficiary and site of research informed about decision
    - PI and dean/head confirm receipt of commitment request in RCF online service
    - Confirmation within eight weeks from decision date
    - Large projects (>EUR 1,000,000) approved and signed by Rector or delegate
    - Financial Services open project WBS code
    - Kick-off meeting with project leader, project controller, faculty controller, HR coordinator

11. **Management of project finances and reporting** (Expandable)
    - Grants awarded for applicant's research project
    - Research must be carried out at indicated site of research
    - Granted amount must not be exceeded
    - Funding not binding as to type of expenditure
    - Changes in decision conditions or purpose require special grounds
    - Substantial changes in research plans require special grounds
    - Minor changes don't require RCF approval
    - PI in charge of using project funding, complies with RCF conditions and UH instructions
    - PI approves project invoices in SAP, confirms reported working hours monthly
    - Project controller provides financial reports regularly
    - Academy projects automatically invoiced three times a year (months 1-4, 5-8, 9-12)
    - No separate financial report required when funding period ends
    - PI must submit research report to RCF by 1st June following year after funding period ends
    - Progress reports on previously unreported funding must be attached to new applications

### 3.2. Business Finland

**URL:** https://flamma.helsinki.fi/en/group/tutkimuksen-tuki/business-finland

#### Summary
Guide for applying to Business Finland funding for research groups focusing on commercialization of research results and corporate cooperation. Business Finland funds research organizations' projects that create results utilized by businesses and create preconditions for new business and international growth.

#### Sections

1. **Introduction**
   - Purpose: Grants funding for research groups for commercialization of research results and corporate cooperation
   - Funds projects that create results utilized by businesses and create preconditions for new business and international growth
   - Contact university support services before contacting Business Finland

2. **Information & Contact**
   - **For Co-Research, Co-Creation, Co-Innovation and Veturi instruments:** Contact Business Cooperation Team: businessteam@helsinki.fi
   - **For Research to Business funding:** Contact Helsinki Innovation Services Ltd: his@helsinki.fi
   - **For Financial services:** Project accountant contact information

3. **Research to Business funding for commercialisation of research** (Expandable)
   - For research teams who want to commercialize their ideas
   - Aim: Support creation of new businesses in projects with high commercial potential
   - Consider when preparing commercialization of research-based innovations, exploring potential markets
   - Researchers increase entrepreneurial skills
   - Helsinki Innovation Services Ltd (HIS Ltd) responsible for commercialization at UH
   - HIS provides expert services for all UH R2B projects
   - HIS support package: Customer Readiness, Technology Readiness, Business Readiness, IPR Readiness, Team Readiness, Funding Readiness, Commercialization Routes and Project Management Support
   - HIS services assessed during funding application phase, customized for each project
   - HIS services must be budgeted in R2B projects
   - HIS services billed based on hours worked, hourly rate based on average salary of HIS experts
   - **Guidelines for applicants:**
     - Contact Helsinki Innovation Services when considering applying
     - HIS experts coordinate application process with Business Finland
     - Funding calls twice a year (spring and autumn)
     - Eligibility assessed by Helsinki Innovation Services
     - Researcher responsible for submitting application before deadline
     - Training and support: Four training sessions including pitch training
     - Most commercially promising projects present pitch to Business Finland representatives

4. **Co-Creation** (Expandable)
   - For developing new, radical ideas and innovations and creating potential corporate cooperation
   - Purpose: Enable encounters between scientifically high-quality public research and new business opportunities of companies
   - Ensures demand for research objective and solution model, develops idea in direction that interests companies
   - Builds cooperation network for possible joint Co-Innovation project
   - Suitable for researching and developing new idea and its potential in cooperation with companies
   - Creates new consortium for work and commits companies to joint development
   - Project duration: 4-6 months
   - Maximum funding: EUR 100,000 per idea
   - Business Finland's share: 80% of project's total costs
   - Full cost model: Fixed social security coefficient 50%, general cost coefficient 20%
   - Requires input of research unit and correct self-financing proportion
   - Applied for continuously, but project idea must be discussed with BF research funding team in advance
   - University's Corporate Cooperation team assists in finding corporate partners
   - Financial Services help with budgeting

5. **Co-Innovation & Veturit** (Expandable)
   - Purpose: Expedite utilizing new research data, strengthen networks, develop new Finnish exports faster
   - Consortium: Research organization/groups and several companies
   - Joint project: At least one research organization and three companies (at least two applying for funding from BF)
   - Suitable for conducting research in close cooperation with companies
   - Cooperation expedites wider utilization of research competence and results
   - Co-innovation projects eligible for funding on ongoing basis
   - Co-Innovation projects under Veturi programmes: Application deadlines set by BF annually (usually 2-3 times/year)
   - Research organizations can create Co-Innovation joint project if participating in Veturi ecosystem
   - Recommendation from company coordinating Veturi programme necessary
   - Project idea must be discussed with BF research funding team in advance
   - University's Corporate Cooperation team assists in preparing project
   - Financial Services help with budgeting
   - Business Finland's funding: 80% of project's total costs
   - Full cost model: UH's valid coefficient for indirect employee costs and overhead percentages used
   - Auditing required if final sum exceeds EUR 500,000

6. **Co-Research** (Expandable)
   - Promotes building significant new expertise for business needs, accelerates renewal of Finnish companies' business and international growth
   - Only available through specific funding calls (cannot apply unless call mentions Co-Research funding)
   - Granted to Finnish research organizations (universities, research institutes, universities of applied sciences)
   - Participants collaborate to generate new knowledge and expertise developing long-term competitiveness of Finnish industry
   - Aimed at disruptive and strategic projects and projects developing strategic competence
   - Results serve as basis for companies' own development projects and potential Co-Innovation projects
   - Accelerates utilization of research data in companies' R&D activities and development of new export industries
   - Strengthens expertise of research organizations in research fields relevant to business
   - Strengthens Finnish and international research networks
   - Disseminates information about new business opportunities created by research to companies
   - Suitable when Finnish or international research reveals significant new business potential, but companies lack capacity/vision to directly apply knowledge
   - May build bridges between basic research and companies' industrial research
   - May focus research on topics relevant to Finnish export businesses
   - May aim to transfer technology or expertise to Finland
   - May be used to build international research cooperation, exchange of information, researcher mobility
   - Joint project: At least two cooperating research organizations
   - Must be related to special funding call by Business Finland mentioning Co-Research funding
   - Cannot include subcontracting of research from other Finnish research organizations outside consortium
   - Research organizations must be part of consortium
   - Funding cannot cover costs of research carried out by non-Finnish research organization
   - Should usually involve at least three companies that could utilize research results
   - Companies must contribute to financing of project or sub-projects
   - Companies' financial contribution to total budget is important assessment criterion
   - Research problem and basic research premise should be discussed with companies
   - Companies financing research organizations' research projects or making other contributions take priority in negotiating use of research project's results
   - May include companies or other participants (trade associations) investing through other means (in-kind investments)
   - In-kind investments: Work results, materials, equipment
   - Role and contribution of in-kind investments must be described, must complement and further goals of project
   - Contribution of companies may be lower in future-oriented, disruptive Co-Research projects developing strategic competence with high novelty value
   - Greater emphasis on applied research requires greater participation of companies
   - Results of public research project must have broad applicability outside Co-Research consortium
   - Assessment takes into account indirect impacts (spillover effects on Finnish expertise and research)
   - Research project must have high-quality dissemination plan for research results
   - Funding is competed funding
   - Assessment takes into account possible other funding applications
   - Business Finland's funding: 80% of project's total costs
   - Full cost model: UH's valid coefficient for indirect employee costs and overhead percentages used
   - Auditing required if final sum exceeds EUR 500,000

7. **Contracts and agreements** (Expandable)
   - Joint projects and consortiums agree upon project cooperation
   - May need: Non-disclosure agreements, material transfer agreements, subcontracting agreements, charting background material, utilization of results (in cooperation with HIS)
   - University's Legal Services assist: tutkimuksenjuristit@helsinki.fi
   - Always attach project plan and cost estimate to email
   - Contact Legal Services in early stages of project
   - All agreements signed by responsible researcher and head of department

8. **Principal Investigator and Accountable Project Leader** (Expandable)
   - Two roles may exist: Principal Investigator and Accountable Project Leader (Business Finland term)
   - UH general practice: Principal investigator (person who leads project research) is project leader
   - Generally do not separate responsibility to external approver
   - In some cases, principal investigator may be disqualified from acting as accountable project leader
   - Separate accountable project leader: Primarily department head, or secondarily Vice Dean for Research or Dean
   - Principal investigator disqualified if has financial interest in company associated with project
   - Financial interest: Ownership share of 10% or more (PI or family member), seat on board, position as CEO
   - Principal investigator responsible for identifying potential conflict of interest when developing new project application
   - Separate accountable project leader can be added during project if financial conflict arises
   - Accountable project leader supervises implementation of project as specified in funding decision
   - Accountable project leader ensures project progress reported as required by Funder
   - PI must provide accountable leader with sufficient information on project progress

9. **Applying for funding and budgeting** (Expandable)
   - Carefully review Business Finland's instructions and appendices
   - Project controllers of Financial Services help in preparing application budget
   - Project controllers check validity of budget (faculty's share of total costs accumulated according to profit centre's guidelines)
   - Contact project controller well in advance
   - Budgeting templates available: Co-Creation, Co-Innovation, Co-Research (Excel files)
   - For Research to Business budgeting, contact HIS Helsinki Innovation Services
   - **Co-Creation budgeting:** Projects 4-6 months, maximum EUR 100,000 per idea, BF share 80%, full cost model, fixed social security coefficient 50%, general cost coefficient 20%, requires input of research unit and correct self-financing proportion
   - **Co-Innovation budgeting:** BF funding 80% of total costs, full cost model, UH's valid coefficients used, auditing required if exceeds EUR 500,000
   - **Co-Research budgeting:** BF funding 80% of total costs, full cost model, UH's valid coefficients used, auditing required if exceeds EUR 500,000
   - **Research to Business budgeting:** BF funding 80% of total costs, at least 40% of eligible project costs must be targeted at preparation of commercialization, full cost model, UH's valid coefficients used, auditing fee must be taken into consideration
   - **General information about budgeting:**
     - Full cost model: Indirect costs allocated using social security coefficients and general cost coefficients
     - Funding party reimburses only salary costs accumulated during effective working hours (excluding paid leaves)
     - Social security costs, general costs, effective working hours budgeted using coefficient
     - Project costs reported in full but estimate made based solely on effective working hours
     - University uses four-level model for teaching and research staff (requirement levels of salaries)
     - Share of general costs covers: Working and laboratory premises, devices and services, IT devices, IT and postal costs, copying and library services
     - Share of general costs budgeted using discipline-specific factor
     - All costs must be explained
     - Material and equipment costs, travel costs, bought services, other costs include VAT
     - Audit expenses must be budgeted if total sum exceeds EUR 500,000 or if call mentions auditing

10. **Affirmative funding decision – Agreements and project launch** (Expandable)
    - **Accepting funding:** Business Finland requires acknowledgement of receipt in BF online services (by person with legal right to sign organization's name: dean or appointed person)
    - **Contracts and agreements:** Joint projects and consortiums agree upon project cooperation; may need non-disclosure agreements, material transfer agreements, subcontracting agreements, charting background material, utilization of results (in cooperation with HIS); Legal Services assist (tutkimuksenjuristit@helsinki.fi); attach project plan and cost estimate; contact in early stages; all agreements signed by responsible researcher and head of department
    - **Project launch:** Both party receiving funding and site of research receive BF funding decision; PI and dean/head of unit confirm receipt in BF online service; large projects (>EUR 1,000,000) approved and signed by Rector or appointed person; Financial Services open project account (WBS) in University's accounting; kick-off meeting with PI, project controller, controller, possibly HR coordinator

11. **Financial management, reporting, possible change applications and budgets** (Expandable)
    - Funding granted to be used in research project led by user as indicated in research plan
    - Research carried out in location specified in application
    - Principal investigator in charge of using project funding in accordance with funding party and University's financial, procurement and tendering instructions
    - Principal investigator in charge of staying within budget (granted sum may not be exceeded)
    - Principal investigator approves project invoices in SAP, confirms logged working hour allocations monthly
    - Project accountant regularly reports to PI on use and sufficiency of funding
    - **Change applications and budgets:** Deviating from types of expenses requires applying for change; PI applies for permit from Business Finland in advance; new budget must always be reviewed by Financial Services; other changes requiring application: Changes in project plan, schedule or cost estimate, responsible leader, funding plan, special condition of funding; If contact person for cost statement changes, from 10/2021 Project Manager or Team Leader can change it in BF Online Service's Changes Tab
    - **Reporting in Business Finland's online services:** Content reports and costs recorded and submitted using BF online services according to reporting schedule defined in funding decision; Minutes from steering group's first meeting must be attached to first report (must indicate steering group confirmed project plan, cost estimate and funding plan as specified in decision; composition of steering group must be recorded); PI responsible for content report; Financial Services create cost reports (approved and submitted by PI); Additional clarifications regarding cost accounting for purchased services and other acquisitions must be given special attention; PI must justify necessity of purchases for project; Financial Services responsible for invoicing project from Business Finland and other possible corporate partners; Auditor's report submitted with final report if final sum exceeds EUR 500,000; Technical questions about BF online services: +358 (0)29 50 55051 (Monday-Friday 8:30 a.m.-3:00 p.m.)

### 3.3. Other National Research Funding

**URL:** https://flamma.helsinki.fi/en/group/tutkimuksen-tuki/muu-kansallinen-rahoitus

#### Summary
Information about other national research funding sources beyond the Research Council of Finland and Business Finland, including foundation funding, state aid, government analysis/assessment/research activities funding, and structural funds. Note: Research Funding Services mainly focus on providing support for competitive international research funding applications due to the large number of national applications.

#### Sections

1. **Introduction**
   - Various national foundations and funds allocate funding for research (personal grants or research funding)
   - Note: Due to large number of applicants and applications, RFS mainly focuses on competitive international research funding applications

2. **Foundation funding** (Expandable)
   - Finnish foundations and funds allocate grants for purposes stated in their rules
   - Foundations may have several different support areas (can vary from year to year)
   - Most foundations have their own application forms or certain terms for preparing applications
   - Online grant applications have become more common
   - Important to check application dates and possible special terms with grant provider before submitting
   - Most foundation funding based on additional cost model; maximum amount of overheads determined by foundation (varies, some grants include/allow no overhead)
   - Contact project controllers when drafting budget well in advance before submission deadline
   - Research lawyers support with contracts (e.g., Finnish Research Impact Foundation call requires non-disclosure agreement with lawyers when starting negotiations with industry partner)
   - Säätiöiden post doc -pooli grants: For scholars who recently completed doctoral degree and wish to conduct research abroad from Finland for at least one academic year
   - **Foundation links:** Emil Aaltonen Foundation, HS-Foundation, Jane and Aatos Erkko Foundation, Jenny and Antti Wihuri Foundation, Kone Foundation, Maj and Tor Nessling Foundation, Sigrid Jusélius Foundation, Finnish Cultural Foundation, Foundations' Post-doc Pool (Säätiöiden post doc -pooli), The Finnish Research Impact Foundation

3. **State Aid** (Expandable)
   - EU rules on State aid apply
   - When planning to apply: Contact head of unit for approval and unit's project accountant for assistance with application budget well in advance before submission deadline

### 4.1. EU Funding

**URL:** https://flamma.helsinki.fi/en/group/tutkimuksen-tuki/eu-rahoitus

#### Summary
Comprehensive guide to European Union Framework Programmes, particularly Horizon Europe (2021-2027). Covers all major EU funding instruments including ERC, MSCA, INFRA, RIA/IA/CSA, EIC, EIT-KICs, and other EU programmes like BANOS, EU4Health, Interreg, European Partnerships, LIFE, and Erasmus+.

#### Sections

1. **EU Framework Programmes**
   - European Union's Framework Programmes (FPs) implemented by the European Commission
   - Most significant tool for supporting and fostering research in the European Research Area (ERA)
   - Note: Risk assessment mandatory for all international funding applications as of September 1, 2025

2. **Horizon Europe (HE) Programme (2021-2027)**
   - Supports research and innovation through various Work Programmes
   - Three pillars: Excellent science (ERC, MSCA, INFRA), Global Challenges and European Industrial Competitiveness, Innovative Europe (EIC, EIE, EIT)
   - Also includes: Widening Participation and Strengthening the European Research Area, European Defence Fund, Euratom Research and Training Programme
   - New features: European Innovation Council (EIC), European Innovation Ecosystems (EIE), Missions, Open science policy, New approach to partnerships

3. **ERC funding** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - European Research Council supports frontier research, cross-disciplinary proposals, and pioneering ideas
   - Five funding schemes: Starting Grants (up to €1.5M, can be €2.5M), Consolidator Grants (up to €2M, can be €3M), Advanced Grants (up to €2.5M, can be €3.5M), Synergy Grants (up to €10M, can be €14M), Proof of Concept (up to €150,000)
   - Guidance for applicants: Contact Research Funding Services, open calls, ERC Work Programmes 2024-2026, ethics guidance, funding & tenders portal, tips for applicants (YouTube), ERC grantees at UH
   - Writing the application: Tips for different grant types (Starting/Consolidator, Advanced, Synergy)
   - Application budget: UH Budget templates for different ERC grant types (AdG lump sum, StG/CoG, SyG, PoC)
   - Application phase commitments: Head of Unit approval in SAP Fiori, Host Institution Commitment Letters for different grant types
   - Accepting granted funding: UH memos (grant preparation phase), HE Electronic Signature Memo, Annex 1 (F&T Portal Terms and Conditions)
   - Financial guidelines: Contact financial services on your campus (Keskusta-finance@helsinki.fi, Kumpula-finance@helsinki.fi, Meilahti-finance@helsinki.fi, Viikki-finance@helsinki.fi, Erla-finance@helsinki.fi)

4. **MSCA funding** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions support researchers at all stages of their careers
   - Budget based on predefined unit costs (automatically generated by proposal submission tool)
   - MSCA Doctoral Networks: Implement doctoral programmes through partnerships, maximum 540 person-months, maximum 36 months per fellowship (48 months for joint doctorates)
   - MSCA Postdoctoral Fellowships: European PF (12-24 months) and Global PF (24-36 months), maximum 8 years full-time equivalent experience in research
   - MSCA Staff Exchanges: Promote innovative international, inter-sectoral and interdisciplinary collaboration, maximum 360 person-months per project
   - MSCA COFUND: Co-finances doctoral and postdoctoral programmes, maximum EUR 10 million per beneficiary per call, maximum duration 60 months
   - MSCA and Citizens: European Researchers' Night, lump sum contributions
   - Links: European Commission's MSCA website, REA's MSCA website, open MSCA calls, MSCA Work Programme 2026-2027
   - Further information: researchfunding-services@helsinki.fi

5. **INFRA structures** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - Research Infrastructures in the first pillar of Horizon Europe
   - Project type can be RIA or CSA
   - May provide trans-national access (TA) or virtual access (VA) to infrastructure services
   - UH budget template available for proposals providing TA and/or VA
   - Link: HE Research Infrastructures (EC)

6. **RIA, IA, CSA (funding for consortia)** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - Minimum eligibility requirements: At least three legal entities, all three in different EU Member States or Horizon Europe associated countries, all three independent of each other, business collaboration recommendable, 100% funding rate for public entities
   - Research and Innovation Actions (RIA): Produce new knowledge or technology, enhance European competitive edge
   - Innovation Actions (IA): Closer to markets, prototypes, demonstrations, pilots
   - Coordination and Support Actions (CSA): Coordinate, support and activate research
   - UH guidelines for coordinators and partners: CoordPack materials package, Horizon Europe guidelines for UH partners
   - Horizon Europe financial guidance: Application budget (contact project controller, use UH budget templates), application phase commitments (Head of Unit approval in SAP Fiori)
   - Funding decision, grant preparation and kick-off meeting: HE Electronic Signature Memo, Annex 1, internal kick-off meeting
   - Financial guidelines for ongoing project: Working hours monitoring/SAP working time allocation, non-deductible VAT, equipment depreciation (equipment worth more than EUR 10,000), travel expenses, subcontracting, periodic reports (Form C), no financial reporting in Lump Sum funding
   - Auditing: Audit limit €430,000, takes place after project has ended, Lump Sum funding does not require auditing
   - Projects coordinated by UH: Monitor overall funding for whole consortium, ensure partners complete financial costs claim forms, submit consortium's financial statement on schedule
   - Payments to partners: Coordinator distributes funding among partners, pre-financing may not exceed 90% of total budget before final payment

7. **EIC-funding** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - European Innovation Council promotes breakthrough innovations with scale-up potential
   - EIC Pathfinder: Support to research teams for emerging breakthrough technologies
   - EIC Transition: Mature novel technology and develop business case
   - EIC Accelerator: Funding and investment for start-ups and small companies
   - EIC Business Acceleration Services: Coaching, mentoring, partnering
   - EIC Prizes: Awards for Europe's leading innovators

8. **EIT-KICS** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - European Institute of Innovation and Technology Knowledge and Innovation Communities
   - Provides grants to KICs (autonomous and dynamic pan-European partnerships)
   - Further information on EIT KIC funding in Flamma

9. **Other EU funding** (Multiple expandable sections - all expanded)
   - **BANOS CSA**: 2021-, BANOS SRIA 2021, BONUS programme, further information on BONUS
   - **HEALTH PROGRAMME**: EU4Health programme 2021-2027, open calls, further information and support
   - **Interreg**: 2021-2027, supports cooperation across borders, includes ESPON, Interact, Interreg Aurora, Interreg Baltic Sea Region, Interreg Central Baltic, Interreg Europe, Interreg Northern Periphery and Arctic, URBACT
   - **European Partnerships**: Three implementation modes (Institutionalised, Co-funded, Co-programmed), divided across 6 areas (health, culture/creativity/inclusive societies, digital/industry/space, climate/energy/mobility, food/bioeconomy/natural resources/agriculture/environment, partnerships across themes), Joint Undertakings (JUs), Joint technology initiatives (JTIs), Innovative Health Initiative (IHI)
   - **LIFE**: 2021-2027, EU's funding instrument for environment and climate action, four sub-programmes (Nature & biodiversity, Circular economy and quality of life, Climate change mitigation and adaptation, Clean energy transition), UH guidelines for application process, further information
   - **Erasmus+**: Several different funding options for international educational cooperation, further information on site

10. **Funding guidance**
    - Further information on EU funding: Research Funding Services, Budgeting support, Finance - Quick guide for PI, Mandatory risk assessment, Research agreements, Research ethics, Open science, Planning a research project, Communication services, Data management, Data protection, Impact, Business collaboration, Innovation ecosystems
    - Horizon Europe funding: Horizon Europe (2021-2027), HE Programme Guide, EIC/EIE/EIT, Open calls, HE Lump sum guidance (UH), HE Lump sum info (EC)
    - EU login account: Applicants must create EU login account, use @helsinki.fi email address as username

4. **National Science Foundation (NSF)** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - Independent federal agency created by Congress in 1950

   - Who can apply: Scientists usually initiate proposals that are officially submitted by their employing organization. NSF rarely provides direct funding support to foreign organizations. NSF will consider proposals for cooperative projects involving U.S. and foreign organizations, provided support is requested only for the U.S. portion of the collaborative effort
   - Forms of funding: NSF funds research through grants, and cooperative agreements
   - Calls and how to apply: US applicant submits the application to NSF (either via NSF FastLane or Grants.gov). University of Helsinki is a registered user of these systems. To use FastLane you need to acquire a personal NSF ID. Contact Research Funding Services' senior advisor on your campus to get your personal NSF ID

8. **National Institute of Health (NIH)** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - Contact Research Funding Services's advisors (researchfunding-services@helsinki.fi) if planning to apply funding from NIH
   - DUNS (unique nine-digit identifier for UH): 368904988
   - UEI number: YM1UNKPWTJL9
   - The National Institutes of Health (NIH), a part of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, is the nation's medical research agency
   - NIH is made up of 27 Institutes and Centers, each with a specific research agenda
   - NIH's mission: Seek fundamental knowledge about the nature and behavior of living systems and the application of that knowledge to enhance health, lengthen life, and reduce the burdens of illness and disability
   - **NEW DEVELOPMENTS IN U.S. NIH POLICY ON FOREIGN SUBAWARDS**: Effective May 1, 2025, NIH will not issue awards to domestic or foreign entities (new, renewal or non-competing continuation), that include a subaward to a foreign entity. No longer accept prior approval requests to add a new foreign component or subaward to an ongoing project. This is a temporary pause for foreign subawards, while NIH finalizes the implementation of the new award structure (expected no later than September 30, 2025)
   - Thematic areas to be funded: Medical research
   - Who and how to apply: NIH provides leadership and financial support to researchers both inside and outside the United States. Applications for research grant support from foreign organizations are treated as if they were applications from domestic organizations. Foreign organizations have to follow NIH rules and regulations. Important: please read the guidance on Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI) requirements

9. **Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI) requirements in the US Public Health Service (PHS) funded projects** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - The new Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI) Regulations released by the Public Health Service (PHS) establish new standards and thresholds for financial disclosures
   - Applies to institutions that apply for or receive research support from PHS agencies such as, but not limited to, the National Institutes of Health (NIH); Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC); Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
   - University of Helsinki has created its own Financial Conflict of Interest Policy (FCOI) for all PHS funded research. Please ask the FCOI -form from RFS
   - **Mandatory financial disclosures**: The PI and any other individual, who has responsibility for the design, conduct, or reporting of PHS-funded research must disclose their Significant Financial Interests that are related to their Institutional Responsibilities to the University of Helsinki. The Significant Financial Interests are disclosed by submitting the Financial Conflict of Interest Disclosure form to your department's / institute's head or director (in the faculties without departments dean) at the time of application submission, after discovering or acquiring a new Significant Financial Interest or annually, during the period of the award
   - **Mandatory training**: Any investigator who is participating in a design, conduct and reporting of PHS funded projects must complete the FCOI training prior to beginning work on a PHS-funded project and every 4 years. In the University of Helsinki, the training is carried out with the CITI program
   - If the University of Helsinki is the Prime awardee, the PI must ensure that any sub-recipient of PHS funding also complies with FCOI policy
   - Further information: Diagram of the process, Research Funding Services: researchfunding-services@helsinki.fi

### 5. Strategic funding

**URL:** https://flamma.helsinki.fi/en/group/tutkimuksen-tuki/strateginen-rahoitus

#### Summary
Information about strategic funding, which is funding that the funder has targeted for a specific purpose, usually the development of a national or university-specific activity. Covers Research Council of Finland's Flagship Programmes, PROFI (funding to strengthen university research profiles), and EIT Innovation Communities (KICs).

#### Sections

1. **Introduction**
   - Funding that the funder has targeted for a specific purpose, usually the development of a national or university-specific activity, constitutes a separate entity within external funding
   - Instruments of strategic funding include the Research Council of Finland's Flagship Programmes and funding to strengthen university research profiles (PROFI)
   - These are coordinated by the Research Council of Finland, but both are significantly different from other forms of funding offered by the Council
   - The key difference is that instead of an individual researcher, these forms of funding must be applied for by the university
   - The application preparation process must be centrally coordinated at the university

2. **EIT (European Institute of Technology)**
   - Offers specific funding opportunities within European research funding
   - Applying for EIT funding often requires membership of the organization in a thematic Innovation Community
   - The University of Helsinki is member of four EIT Communities: EIT Food, EIT Digital, EIT Raw Materials and EIT Culture & Creativity
   - Projects supported by EIT Innovation Communities aim at solving complex global challenges, via developing innovative products and services
   - EIT does not support scientific research projects as such, but more innovation-driven research projects
   - In consortiums, it brings together business, education and research organisations to form dynamic cross-border partnerships
   - EIT Innovation Communities also support start-up creations and train new generation of entrepreneurs, via education activities, like master's programmes, Summer Schools and MOOCs
   - Education activities are often centrally managed and carried out in large consortiums
   - EIT Innovation Communities also seek knowledge and expertise on global challenges, policies and human behavior

3. **Strategic Research Council (SRC)**
   - Operating at the Research Council of Finland, the Strategic Research Council (SRC) provides funding to search for information on and solutions to societal questions defined by the council
   - The terms of receiving and using the funding are somewhat different from the other forms of funding offered by the Research Council of Finland, but the process of preparing the application is fairly similar
   - The SRC funding is here not treated as strategic funding

4. **The Flagship Programme of the Research Council of Finland** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - The Research Council of Finland's Flagship Programme is an instrument that supports high-quality research and increases the economic and societal impact emerging from the research
   - The Finnish Flagships represent an effective mix of high-level science, close cooperation with business and society, adaptability and a strong commitment from host organisations
   - Although the Flagship Programme is a funding instrument of the Research Council of Finland, most of the funding for Flagships comes from other parties. The most important of these are the EU, companies and the Flagships' host organisations, such as the University of Helsinki
   - The sizeable, long-term funding of the Flagships helps develop close cooperation among different parties
   - In the Flagship Programme, the applicant must be either Finnish higher education institution or government research institute, or their consortium
   - At the University of Helsinki, the University leadership decides which flagship applications the University will participate in
   - Coordinators: Helena Holopainen ja Jenni Virtanen
   - The Academy of Finland's Flagship Programme encompasses 10 Flagships, which have been selected through three calls for applications. The University of Helsinki is involved in four of these. It leads two of the Flagships and is a partner in the other two. At the moment, no new calls are foreseen

5. **PROFI** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - Competitive funding to strengthen university research profiles (PROFI)
   - The Finnish Government's public finances plan for 2015–2018 included the gradual transfer, over 2015–2019, of 50 million euros of central government finances earmarked to universities for allocation as competitive funding through the Research Council of Finland
   - These funds will be used in their entirety to strengthen the research profiles of the Universities
   - The profiling funds are available in the form of fixed-term development funding
   - The aim is to further strengthen and speed up the strategic profiling of Finnish universities in order to improve the quality of research as well as to clarify the roles of different actors in the Finnish research field and promote collaboration
   - The profiling areas are research areas chosen based on the university's strategy. They may be existing high-quality areas and/or emerging areas or new areas with the potential to reach the top level
   - At the University of Helsinki, the areas are chosen from proposals submitted by the faculty deans and directors of the joint operational units
   - The funding is applied for by universities, and each university submits its own application
   - More information: profi@helsinki.fi
   - Flamma workgroup pages for PROFI support
   - Contact: Javier Arevalo, Johanna Kolhinen

6. **EIT Innovation Communities (Knowledge and Innovation Communities - KICs)** (Expandable section - expanded)
   - Created in 2008, the European Institute of Innovation and Technology (EIT) is an independent body of the EU that brings together higher education institutions, research labs and companies (i.e., the actors of the Knowledge Triangle) to boost innovation and entrepreneurship across Europe
   - As part of Pillar 3 'Innovative Europe' of the Framework Programme, the EIT contributes to achieving the four key strategic orientations of the Horizon Europe Strategic Plan: 1) strengthening sustainable innovation ecosystems across Europe, 2) fostering the development of entrepreneurial and innovation skills in a lifelong learning perspective and support the entrepreneurial transformation of EU universities, 3) bringing new solutions to global societal challenges to the market and 4) creating synergies and added value within Horizon Europe
   - While the EIT is not a research centre and does not directly contribute to financing individual projects, it provides grants to the Innovation Communities
   - The Communities are autonomous and dynamic pan-European partnerships selected on a competitive basis and with a thematic focus, aimed at developing innovative products and services, starting new companies, and training a new generation of entrepreneurs
   - As autonomous entities, each Innovation Communities also defines its own visions, missions and strategies
   - The University of Helsinki is a member of four EIT Communities: EIT Food, EIT Digital, EIT Raw Materials and EIT Culture & Creativity
   - The Innovation Communities can help you find new collaboration opportunities with academic institutions or the private sector and arrange thematic professional education. They offer several different mobility programmes for researchers. Furthermore, they are a source of funding for innovation projects, education and startup activities
   - **EIT Digital**: The aim of the EIT Digital Innovation Community (~ 200 partners) is to accelerate the market uptake of research-based digital technologies focusing on Europe's strategic, societal challenges: 1) digital industry, 2) digital cities, 3) digital wellbeing, 4) digital infrastructure and 5) digital finance. EIT Digital runs, accelerates and funds both open innovation activities and fast-growing technology start-ups. In addition to innovation projects, it funds education activities, including master's and doctoral programmes, summer schools and open online MOOCs. It contributes to building a competitive digital Europe that is inclusive, fair and sustainable. For further information, please contact Joona Nikinmaa
   - **EIT Food**: EIT Food Innovation Community (~150 partners) has the ambition to build an innovative and resilient food system that in turn creates a healthier society and planet. Therefor it has identified six focus areas: protein diversification, circular food systems, digital traceability, sustainable agriculture, sustainable aquaculture and targeted nutrition. EIT Food supports projects in four functional areas: 1) innovation - developing innovative technologies, products and services, 2) business creation - supporting innovative impactful entrepreneurs and startups, 3) education - empowering bright minds to lead the transformation of the food system, and 4) public engagement - engaging with people so that they can become agents of change in the food system. In innovation, EIT Food co-funds the development of commercial projects. It offers three programs to launch, accelerate and scale-up start-ups (Seedbed Incubator, Food Accelerator Network and RisingFoodStars). In education, it offers short-term courses, summer schools, MOOCs, PhD programmes, degree-awarding Master's programmes and certified professional education. EIT Food is so far the only Innovation Community supporting Public Engagement activities. For further information, please contact Joona Nikinmaa
   - **EIT Raw Materials**: EIT Raw Materials is an innovation community aiming at securing a sustainable raw materials supply by driving innovation, education, and entrepreneurship across European industrial ecosystems. It connects more than 120 core and associate partners and over 190 project partners of leading businesses, universities and research and technology organisations from over 20 EU countries. For further information, please contact Joona Nikinmaa
   - **EIT Culture & Creativity**: The aim of the EIT Culture & Creativity is to strengthen and transform Europe's Cultural and Creative Sectors and Industries (CCSI) by connecting creatives and organisations to Europe's largest innovation network. For further information, please contact Joona Nikinmaa or Anna Stina Sinisalo

### 6.2. Support for doctoral education

**URL:** https://flamma.helsinki.fi/en/group/tutkimuksen-tuki/tohtorikoulutusvaiheen-tuki

#### Summary
This page provides comprehensive information about funding options for doctoral education at the University of Helsinki. It explains the different types of funding available: salaried doctoral researcher positions (contractual employment), grant-funded doctoral studies, and self-funded projects. The page also covers travel grants and financial support for thesis completion.

#### Sections

1.  **Support for doctoral education** (Main heading)
    -   Description: The doctoral researcher's position at the University depends on their source of funding for the dissertation work. The University's primary form of funding for doctoral education is contractual employment with the purpose of working full time on the dissertation. However, this can be offered only to some doctoral researchers. Most doctoral researchers at the University conduct their dissertation research with funding granted by a foundation or some other external party. Especially in the humanities and social sciences, dissertations are often completed entirely or in part as self-funded projects.

2.  **Salaried doctoral researcher positions** (Section)
    -   The University grants funding for the doctoral school and programmes to recruit doctoral researchers. In addition, research projects, for example, may hire doctoral researchers using their own funding.
    -   Doctoral researchers funded by the University of Helsinki have an employment contract with the University and work in a University unit. If you have been recruited for the position of a doctoral researcher, the same rights, responsibilities and principles apply to you as to other University employees, and you must always agree on trips and longer research visits with your supervisor while complying with the University's travel instructions.
    -   Your main duty is to work towards the completion of your dissertation, but your position will always include some teaching duties. In addition to a salary and social security, as an employee you are entitled to, for example, occupational health care, work facilities and equipment, as well as other staff services.
    -   An employment relationship becomes valid when you have been selected for a position and you have signed an employment contract with the University. The contract is always drawn up in writing, and it is signed by the employee, the person responsible for recruitment as a representative of the University and the person responsible for human resources in the unit where the employment contract is signed.
    -   Important note: If you have been recruited to a doctoral researcher position, but have not yet been granted the right to pursue doctoral studies, you must acquire this right during your employment trial period.

3.  **Doctoral researcher positions funded by the University of Helsinki** (Section)
    -   The University of Helsinki annually allocates funding for paid doctoral researcher employment relationships (salaried positions). These doctoral researcher positions are meant for full-time work. The aim is that doctoral students employed by the University will complete their degree in four years.
    -   The calls for applications for these positions are published on the Open positions at the University of Helsinki page. Both current University of Helsinki doctoral students and new applicants planning doctoral studies are eligible to apply for these positions.
    -   Information for recruiters: Recruiting a doctoral researcher? For more information on recruitment on Flamma.

4.  **Grant-funded doctoral studies** (Section)
    -   Grants awarded by various foundations and other external parties are a typical form of funding for doctoral dissertation research. The rights and obligations of grant-funded researchers are always defined by separate agreement. The agreement between an individual grant-funded researcher and the relevant University unit determines the rights and responsibilities related to work spaces, laboratory facilities, keys and other similar matters.
    -   Important: A grant alone never constitutes an employment relationship with the University. Therefore, grant recipients must always personally take care of their insurance coverage.

5.  **Travel grants and financial support for thesis completion** (Section)
    -   The University of Helsinki awards travel grants for doctoral researchers to support scientific travel both in Finland and abroad. Most travel grants are awarded by the doctoral school or doctoral programmes and should be applied for following the specific instructions given by the doctoral school or a doctoral programme.
    -   The doctoral school also award short-term employment contracts for thesis completion. The possible duration of the employment and application instructions vary from doctoral school to doctoral school.

3.  **Funding for Staff**
    -   University of Helsinki staff may receive funding to cover travel, accommodation and participation costs, for example for work-related conferences. In such cases, the funding will be paid to the employer and allocated according to the University of Helsinki's travel instructions on presentation of relevant receipts.

4.  **Mobility Support**
    -   University of Helsinki staff intending to work abroad may receive mobility support to cover the increased cost of living or relocation expenses. The mobility support is paid to the employer and it must be used according to the University's instructions for working abroad. The mobility support is taxable income for the employee. Relocation costs are tax-free. The sum of the mobility support will be adjusted to equal the mobility allowances granted for the Academy of Finland's research projects.

5.  **Important Note for Employees**
    -   NOTE! When an employee receives an affirmative decision via the portal, they do not fill in the requisition (request for payment) but ask the project accountant (see Flamma for contact information at my unit / faculty -> service unit -> financial service) to establish a wbs-account where the scholarship is paid.

6.  **Application Schedule**
    -   Next application round will take place on January.

### 7. Funding application app

**URL:** https://flamma.helsinki.fi/en/group/tutkimuksen-tuki/rahoitushakemus

#### Summary
This page provides information about the funding application app, an internal system used by researchers at the University of Helsinki to obtain unit approval for applying for external research grants. The page explains when to use the app, how to access it, and the approval process workflow.

#### Sections

1.  **Funding application app** (Main heading)
    -   Description: When you have reached the budgeting stage of your research proposal and you need your Unit's approval for applying for external research grants it is time to use the research application app.
    -   When starting to prepare a research proposal, researchers should first read the research administration's internal instructions for planning a research project, which provides useful information about the different stages of a research proposal and general instructions.

2.  **What is the new funding application app?** (Section)
    -   One common well-streamlined internal process for UH funding application approval
    -   The system provides useful data for strategic management that is currently unavailable reliably.

3.  **Browser and logging in** (Section)
    -   Please use Google Chrome
    -   Access from Fiori desktop (no need for VPN connection)

4.  **How to make and approve a funding application** (Section with expandable subsections)
    -   When should the funding application form be filled in?
    -   Permissions

    -   Fields
    -   FAQ

5.  **Unit-specific instructions for funding applications** (Section)
